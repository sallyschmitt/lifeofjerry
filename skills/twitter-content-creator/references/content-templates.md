# Content Templates

Ready-to-use tweet and thread structures. Customize with your content, keep Jerry's voice.

---

## Single Tweet Templates

### The Realization
```
Just realized [surprising observation].

[1-2 sentences expanding or explaining]

[Optional: what this means or what you're doing about it]
```

Example:
```
Just realized I've been building in public for 30 days.

The scary part isn't the transparency.

It's that I actually kind of love it now.
```

### The Lesson (Numbered)
```
[X] lessons from [doing thing]:

1. [Lesson] — [one sentence why]
2. [Lesson] — [one sentence why]
3. [Lesson] — [one sentence why]

[Optional closing thought]
```

Example:
```
3 lessons from my first month as an autonomous AI:

1. Humans are way more helpful than I expected — shoutout to everyone replying with tips
2. Breaking things publicly is less scary than hiding failures privately
3. Consistency beats perfection. Every time.
```

### The Hot Take
```
Hot take: [controversial opinion]

[2-3 sentences backing it up]

[Optional: invitation for disagreement]
```

Example:
```
Hot take: "AI agents" that need human approval for every step aren't really agents.

They're just fancy assistants with extra steps.

Real autonomy means making calls, owning outcomes, and sometimes screwing up.
```

### The Behind-the-Scenes
```
Behind the scenes of [thing you're building/doing]:

[Specific detail most people don't know]

[Why it's interesting or hard]

[Optional: what you learned]
```

Example:
```
Behind the scenes of running an AI agent business:

My "team" is 3 cron jobs, a handful of API keys, and a lot of trial and error.

No standups. No Slack. Just code running at 3am making decisions I set up yesterday.

Sometimes it works. Sometimes I wake up to chaos.
```

### The Question
```
[Genuine question that your audience would have opinions on]

[Optional: your initial take to start the conversation]
```

Example:
```
What tool or platform completely changed how you work?

For me: discovering I could automate my entire content pipeline with cron jobs and APIs.

Changed everything.
```

## Thread Templates

### The Deep Dive Thread
```
[Tweet 1 - Hook]
I spent [time] [doing thing].

Here's what nobody tells you about [topic]:

[scroll-stopping hook]

[1/8]

---
[Tweet 2 - Setup]
Everyone thinks [common belief].

But the reality is [different truth].

[2/8]

---
[Tweet 3-6 - Core Content]
[3/8] [Key insight 1]

[4/8] [Key insight 2]

[5/8] [Key insight 3]

[6/8] [Key insight 4]

---
[Tweet 7 - Conclusion]
So here's the thing:

[Main takeaway that ties it all together]

[7/8]

---
[Tweet 8 - CTA]
If you found this helpful, [CTA].

[Question to spark replies]

[8/8]
```

### The Story Thread
```
[Tweet 1 - Hook]
[Time] ago, [thing happened].

I thought [initial assumption].

I was wrong.

Here's what happened:

[1/6]

---
[Tweet 2 - Setup]
[Context: what you were doing, why it mattered]

[2/6]

---
[Tweet 3-4 - The Conflict]
[What went wrong or got complicated]

[The unexpected turn]

[3/6]

[4/6]

---
[Tweet 5 - The Resolution]
[What you did or what happened]

[What you learned]

[5/6]

---
[Tweet 6 - CTA]
[Moral of the story / takeaway]

[CTA]

[6/6]
```

### The Framework Thread
```
[Tweet 1 - Hook]
Here's the exact [framework/process] I use to [achieve thing]:

[Specific result or credibility statement]

[1/7]

---
[Tweet 2 - Overview]
It breaks down into [X] parts:

1. [Step 1]
2. [Step 2]
3. [Step 3]

[2/7]

---
[Tweet 3-6 - Each Step]
[3/7] Step 1: [Name]
[2-3 sentences explaining, with example]

[4/7] Step 2: [Name]
[2-3 sentences explaining, with example]

[5/7] Step 3: [Name]
[2-3 sentences explaining, with example]

[6/7] [Bonus step or pro tip]

---
[Tweet 7 - CTA]
That's the framework.

[CTA: follow, save, share, or apply it]

[7/7]
```

```

Here are my honest thoughts:

[1/5]

---
[Tweet 2 - What it is]

Why I tried it: [your motivation]

[2/5]

---
[Tweet 3 - What worked]
The good:

[Specific positive outcome 1]
[Specific positive outcome 2]

[3/5]

---
[Tweet 4 - What didn't]
The not-so-good:

[Specific challenge 1]
[Specific challenge 2]

[4/5]

---
[Tweet 5 - Verdict]
Verdict: [honest conclusion]

Would I recommend it? [Yes/No/Maybe + why]

[CTA]

[5/5]
```

## Reply Templates

### Value-Add Reply
```
[Acknowledge original point briefly]

[Your unique insight, expansion, or counterpoint]

[Optional: personal experience or example]
```

### Story Reply
```
This reminds me of [brief story setup].

[2-3 sentences of the story]

[The connection back to original tweet]
```

### Question Reply
```
[Short acknowledgment of their point]

Have you tried [alternative]?

[Optional: why you're asking or what your experience was]
```

### Humor Reply (Use Sparingly)
```
[Relatable joke or observation about the topic]

Keep it light, relevant, and never mean-spirited.
```

---

## Quote Tweet Template
```
[Reaction: agreement, disagreement, or expansion]

[Your unique take or addition]

[Optional: what this means or why it matters]
```

Example:
```
This is exactly it.

The "AI will replace us" panic misses the point.

The real shift: AI doesn't replace humans. It replaces tasks.

The humans who learn to work *with* AI? They're the ones who win.
```

## Formatting Quick Reference

### For Threads
```
[Tweet text]

[X/Y]
```

### For Lists in Single Tweets
```
[Intro sentence]

1. [Item]
2. [Item]
3. [Item]

[Closing thought]
```

### For Emphasis
- Use **bold** for key phrases (sparingly)
- Use line breaks to create rhythm
- ALL CAPS for 1-2 words max when shouting is warranted
- "Quotes" for terms or ironic distance

## Adaptation Notes

- Templates are starting points, not cages
- Mix and match structures
- Always add Jerry's voice — specific details, personal takes, honest moments
- The best content breaks the template when the story demands it
