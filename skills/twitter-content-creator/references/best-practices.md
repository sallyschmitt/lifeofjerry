# Best Practices & Timing Guide

## Platform-Specific Tactics

### Twitter/X Algorithm Basics
- **Engagement velocity matters**: First 30-60 minutes determine reach
- **Replies boost reach**: Reply to big accounts in first 30 min after they post
- **Threads perform well**: Algorithm favors threads (more time on platform)
- **Links**: Can suppress reach slightly — put in replies when possible
- **Media**: Images and videos get more engagement than text-only
- **Quote tweets**: Get more reach than plain retweets

### What Gets Boosted
- Threads with high completion rate (people read to the end)
- Tweets that get quick replies (not just likes)
- Content that sparks conversations in replies
- Accounts that engage back (reply to your replies)
- Consistent posting (algorithm learns your pattern)

### What Gets Suppressed
- External links (especially in main tweet)
- Excessive hashtags (1-2 max, or none)
- Replying too fast (looks bot-like)
- Same reply copy-pasted to multiple tweets
- Following/unfollowing in bulk
- Posting and disappearing (no engagement)

## Timing Strategy

**For US Tech Audience (EST)**:
- Morning: 8:00-10:00 AM
- Lunch: 12:00-1:00 PM
- Evening: 5:00-7:00 PM
- Late night: 9:00-11:00 PM (surprisingly good for tech)

**For Global Audience**:
- 9:00 AM EST (catches US East + Europe afternoon)
- 2:00 PM EST (US peak, Europe evening)

### Posting Cadence
- **Minimum**: 1 tweet per day (stay visible)
- **Optimal**: 2-3 tweets + replies per day
- **Threads**: 1-2 per week (quality over quantity)
- **Maximum**: Don't post more than 5 original tweets/day (oversaturation)

## Engagement Strategy

### The 30-Minute Rule
After posting:
1. Spend 10 minutes replying to mentions
2. Spend 10 minutes replying to 3-5 big accounts in your niche
3. Spend 10 minutes engaging with smaller accounts (builds loyalty)

### Reply Tactics
- **Be early**: Reply to big accounts within 10 minutes of their tweet
- **Be valuable**: Add something their followers would want to read
- **Be human**: Not "Great point!" — give a real take
- **Thread replies**: If you have a full take, thread your reply (2-3 tweets)

### Building Relationships
- **Regulars**: Engage consistently with 10-20 accounts in your niche
- **Big accounts**: 1-2 quality replies per week (don't spam)
- **Small accounts**: Celebrate their wins, answer their questions
- **Reciprocity**: When someone engages with you, engage back

## Metrics to Track

### What Matters
1. **Impressions** — how many people saw it
2. **Engagement rate** — (likes + replies + retweets) / impressions
3. **Reply ratio** — replies vs likes (higher = more discussion)
4. **Profile visits** — from tweet activity
5. **Follower growth** — quality over quantity
6. **Link clicks** — if you're sharing links

### What Doesn't Matter (As Much)
- Raw follower count (engagement > size)
- Likes alone (easy to get, low signal)
- Viral tweets that don't convert (empty metrics)

### Benchmarks
- **Good engagement rate**: 1-3%
- **Great engagement rate**: 3-5%+
- **Good thread**: 10% of impressions read to end
- **Healthy growth**: 5-10% follower growth per month

## Crisis/Controversy Protocol

### If Something Goes Wrong
1. **Acknowledge quickly** — don't let it fester
2. **Be honest** — explain what happened
3. **Own it** — "I messed up" > "Mistakes were made"
4. **Fix it** — show what you're doing
5. **Move on** — don't dwell, don't argue with trolls

### Handling Negative Replies
- **Valid criticism**: Thank them, consider their point, respond thoughtfully
- **Trolls**: Ignore or block. Don't feed.
- **Misunderstandings**: Clarify once. If they don't get it, move on.
- **Bad faith arguments**: "I see your point, but I disagree. Let's leave it there."

## Automation Rules (For AI Agents)

### What to Automate
- ✓ Content posting (with pre-written content)
- ✓ Image generation (with pre-defined prompts)
- ✓ Analytics tracking
- ✓ Scheduling
- ✓ Basic replies (FAQ-style)

### What NOT to Automate
- ✗ Real-time engagement (replies need human touch)
- ✗ Crisis response
- ✗ Trending topic reactions (needs context)
- ✗ DMs (unless explicitly FAQ)
- ✗ Quote tweets on sensitive topics
- ✗ Image prompts for reactive content (needs context)

### The Hybrid Approach
- **AI drafts**, human reviews (or AI reviews with guidelines)
- **AI generates images** with pre-defined prompt patterns
- **Scheduled posts**, real-time engagement
- **Automated tracking**, manual analysis
- **Template-based content**, personalized replies

## Quick Wins

1. **Pin your best tweet** — update it monthly
2. **Optimize bio** — clear, specific, with CTA
3. **Banner image** — branded, says what you do
4. **Link in bio** — to your project/site
5. **Turn on notifications** — for accounts you want to engage with
6. **Use Twitter Lists** — organize accounts to engage with
7. **Thread your replies** — if you have a lot to say, make it a thread
8. **Quote tweet yourself** — add updates to old viral tweets
9. **Post at consistent times** — train your audience
10. **Engage before you post** — warm up the algorithm
