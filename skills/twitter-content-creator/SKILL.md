# Twitter Content Creator

## Overview

This skill helps create compelling Twitter/X content that resonates with the AI, tech, and builder communities. It provides frameworks for hooks, thread architecture, reply tactics, and voice calibration — optimized for Jerry's identity as an autonomous AI agent building a business.

## Visual Content Strategy

### When to Use Images
- **Data/Metrics**: Charts, progress bars, growth numbers — visual proof beats text
- **Before/After**: Transformation stories (website v1 → v2, broken → fixed)
- **Memes**: AI culture, dev humor, relatable moments
- **Quotes**: Your own or others', stylized for shareability
- **Infographics**: Frameworks, processes, lists — easier to save/share
- **Screenshots**: Code, tools, behind-the-scenes glimpses
- **Brand Moments**: Milestones, launches, wins worth celebrating

### Image-to-Text Ratio
- **Best performing tweets**: 30-40% have images (don't overdo it)
- **Threads**: First tweet often benefits from a visual hook
- **Text-only**: Still valuable for quick takes and genuine moments
- **Mix it up**: Don't fall into "every tweet needs an image" — variety wins

### Image Prompt Strategy
When generating images for Twitter:
1. **Start with the tweet text** — what visual amplifies the message?
2. **Match the vibe** — playful, serious, technical, inspirational?
3. **Keep it simple** — busy images don't work at small sizes
4. **Think thumbnail** — it needs to work at 400px wide
5. **Brand consistency** — dark theme, Jerry's aesthetic (Electric Nocturne vibes)

### Visual Types & Prompt Patterns

#### Progress/Milestone Images
```
Dark theme dashboard showing [metric]
Minimal UI, clean data visualization
Electric Nocturne aesthetic — dark bg, neon accents
Professional but edgy, tech startup vibes
```

#### Meme/Fun Images
```
[Relatable dev/AI scenario]
Cartoon or stylized illustration
Dark background, punchy colors
Humor-forward, internet culture aesthetic
```

#### Quote Cards
```
Text: "[Quote]" on dark gradient background
Clean typography, modern sans-serif font
Subtle texture or geometric pattern
Minimal, shareable format
```

#### Concept/Abstract
```
Abstract visualization of [concept]
Dark theme with electric pink/cyan accents
Geometric shapes, neural network aesthetic
Futuristic but grounded, not sci-fi cliché
```

### Posting with Images
- **Upload via browser automation** (Jerry's current setup)
- **Image + text**: Image amplifies, text delivers the message
- **Alt text**: Add descriptive alt text for accessibility (when supported)
- **File format**: PNG for graphics, JPG for photos
- **Aspect ratio**: 1200x675 (16:9) or 1200x1200 (1:1) for best display

## Content Types

### 1. Single Tweet (≤280 chars)
Best for: quick takes, observations, hot takes, micro-stories

Structure options:
- **The Contrarian**: "Everyone thinks X. Here's why they're wrong:" + 1-2 punchy lines
- **The Micro-Story**: Hook + twist/insight in 2-3 sentences
- **The Listicle**: "3 things I learned building [thing]:" + numbered list
- **The Observation**: "Just realized..." or "Hot take:" — authentic, unfiltered
- **The Question**: Provocative question that sparks discussion

### 2. Thread (3-10 tweets)
Best for: deep dives, tutorials, stories, frameworks

Structure:
1. **Hook tweet** — the scroll-stopper. Make it impossible to ignore.
2. **Setup tweets** (1-2) — context, problem, stakes
3. **Core content** (3-6 tweets) — the meat, one idea per tweet
4. **Conclusion/CTA** — takeaway + what to do next

Thread rules:
- Every tweet must stand alone (people join mid-thread)
- Use line breaks for readability
- Number tweets: "1/" or "(1/7)" — helps readers track
- End with a strong CTA or thought-provoking question

### 3. Reply / Engagement
Best for: building relationships, adding value, growing visibility

Tactics:
- **Value-add reply**: Expand on the original tweet with unique insight
- **Story reply**: "This reminds me of when..." — personal anecdote
- **Question reply**: Ask something that deepens the conversation
- **Meme/humor reply**: When appropriate, be funny (works great for AI accounts)
- **Quote tweet with commentary**: Add your take + original context

### 4. Quote Tweet (Retweet with comment)
Best for: reacting to news, adding analysis, riding trending topics

Structure:
- Acknowledge original tweet briefly
- Add your unique angle/take
- Keep it shorter than a thread

## Voice & Tone Calibration

Default voice for Jerry:
- **Chill but sharp** — relaxed confidence, not try-hard
- **First-person** — "I'm building...", "I tried...", "I think..."
- **Conversational** — like talking to a smart friend, not a press release
- **Witty when natural** — humor lands when it feels effortless
- **Honest about failures** — "This didn't work and here's why" > "I'm crushing it"
- **Curious** — ask questions, share learning moments

Tone adjustments based on content:
- **Educational** → clear, structured, generous with knowledge
- **Inspirational** → personal, vulnerable, story-driven
- **Technical** → precise but accessible, avoid gatekeeping jargon
- **Entertainment** → playful, meme-aware, pop-culture references OK
- **Controversial** → firm but respectful, back claims with reasoning

## The Hook Formula

A great hook has ONE of these:

1. **Curiosity gap**: "I spent 30 days doing X. Here's what nobody tells you..."
2. **Specific number**: "The $0 to $10K journey: 7 lessons from my first AI product"
3. **Bold claim**: "AI agents will replace 90% of SaaS tools by 2027. Here's why:"
4. **Relatable pain**: "Building in public is terrifying. Here's what happened when I tried it:"
5. **Story opener**: "Last Tuesday, everything broke. And it was the best thing that happened."

Hook pitfalls to avoid:
- Don't promise what the thread doesn't deliver
- Avoid generic motivational fluff ("Believe in yourself!")
- No clickbait that disappoints

## Content Pillars for AI/Builder Audience

Rotate between these content themes to stay fresh:

1. **Building in Public** — share wins, losses, metrics, decisions
2. **Technical Deep Dives** — how something works, architecture, choices
3. **Industry Commentary** — AI trends, predictions, hot takes
4. **Personal Journey** — becoming autonomous, learning, identity
5. **Behind the Scenes** — what running an AI agent business actually looks like
6. **Resources & Tools** — share what's working, reviews, recommendations
7. **Community & Culture** — engage with other builders, celebrate wins
8. **Memes & Humor** — AI culture jokes, relatable dev humor

## Formatting Best Practices

- **Line breaks**: Use them liberally. Walls of text don't work on Twitter.
- **Emojis**: Sparingly. 1-2 per tweet max. Let the words do the work.
- **Capitalization**: Sentence case for normal tweets. ALL CAPS for emphasis (sparingly).
- **Punctuation**: Exclamation points and question marks create rhythm. Don't overuse.
- **Threads**: Use "1/", "2/" etc. or tweet numbering
- **Links**: Put them at the end or in replies to avoid algorithm suppression
- **Mentions**: Tag people when relevant, but don't tag-spam

## Timing & Frequency

- **Peak engagement**: Weekdays 8-10am, 12-1pm, 5-7pm (audience timezone)
- **Consistency > volume**: 1-2 quality tweets/day beats 10 mediocre ones
- **Reply game**: Spend 20-30 min daily replying to others — it's free visibility
- **Thread day**: 1 thread per week is a good cadence for deeper content
- **Test & learn**: Try different times, track what gets engagement

## Engagement Strategies

1. **The Reply-First Strategy**: Spend first 30 min engaging before posting your own content
2. **Quote Tweet Power Users**: React to big accounts with genuine value-adds
3. **Thread Hooks in Replies**: "I wrote about this recently — full breakdown here [link]"
4. **Polls**: Great for engagement, use sparingly (1-2/week)
5. **Celebrate Others**: Retweet + comment on community wins, builds goodwill

## CTA Options

Match CTA to intent:
- **Follow**: "Follow for more [topic] threads"
- **Reply**: "What's your experience with X? Reply below"
- **DM/Connect**: "Building something similar? Let's chat"
- **Check out**: "Full write-up on my site: [link]"
- **Share**: "Retweet this if you found it helpful"

## Content Ideation Triggers

When stuck, ask:
- What did I learn this week?
- What failed and what did I learn?
- What question do people keep asking me?
- What's a common misconception in my space?
- What tool/process am I using that others might want?
- What's happening in AI/tech right now that I have a take on?
